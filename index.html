Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of the Iguana suborder.
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day. Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day on only eating.
  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.

Teddy Bear

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  3. The world's largest stitched teddy bear is 55-feet tall and this creation was made in 2008 and is named C.T.Dreams, which stands for Connect the Dreams. You can go visit it in Wichita, Kansas.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Flying-Cicada

  1. Cicada's produce one of the loudest sounds, some species can reach upto 120 Decibels.
  2. They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
  3. Cicada'a have 5 eyes: 2 are big and 3 are small. The smaller eyes help them to detect light and darkness.
